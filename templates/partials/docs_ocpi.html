07 OCPI Roaming — Connecting to Other Networks OCPI 2.2.1

OCPI (Open Charge Point Interface) is the protocol that lets charging networks talk to each other. When a Shell Recharge subscriber drives to your chargers, OCPI is how their app knows your charger is available, how their token gets authorized, and how the session data gets back to their billing system. Without it, you're an island. With it, you're part of the European charging network.

Push vs Pull — How Data Flows

OCPI supports two synchronization modes. In practice, most deployments use push for performance:

Push (Preferred)
Data is sent to the partner immediately when it changes. A charger goes offline → LocationsUpdate pushed to all eMSPs within seconds. Lower latency, lower overhead. Requires your server to be reachable by partners.
Pull (Fallback)
Partner polls your endpoint on a schedule (typically every 5–15 minutes). Used when push isn't available or for initial synchronization. Results in stale availability data in partner apps.

Setting Up a Roaming Partner

  1. Navigate to OCPI Roaming in the admin sidebar.
  2. Click Add Partner. Enter the partner's OCPI versions URL (e.g., https://ocpi.shell.com/ocpi/versions).
  3. OpenCPO performs the credential handshake automatically: it fetches the versions endpoint, discovers the 2.2.1 module endpoints, then posts credentials (its own token + identity).
  4. The partner responds with their credential token. Both sides are now authenticated.
  5. OpenCPO immediately pushes your locations to the partner. Their drivers can see your chargers within minutes.
  6. Set a roaming tariff: Tariffs & Pricing → New Tariff → assign to OCPI partner. This is what the eMSP uses to bill their driver.

The full OCPI credentials exchange (steps 2–4) takes under 30 seconds. If it fails, the OCPI log shows exactly which request failed and why — check Admin → OCPI Roaming → Log.

Roaming Tariffs

You set the price you charge eMSP drivers. This differs from what drivers see in the eMSP app — that's between them and their eMSP. Your tariff is the B2B wholesale rate.

Typical structure: energy rate (€/kWh) + optional idle fee (€/min after X minutes). Some operators add a flat session fee. Keep it simple — complex tariffs with time-of-day components create CDR disputes.

Tip: Set your roaming tariff 10–20% above your public tariff to account for settlement delays and the eMSP's margin. You receive payment from the eMSP (net of their platform fee) typically 30–60 days after the session.
