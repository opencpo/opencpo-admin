09 Financial Incentives & Subsidies Europe

The economics of EV charging infrastructure are substantially improved by national and regional subsidy programs. A 120 kW DC charger installation that costs €80,000 all-in can become a €45,000 net investment after subsidies — and then generate recurring revenue through energy sales and CO₂ certificates from day one. This section covers verified programs available in early 2026.

Disclaimer: Information reflects programs available in early 2026. Subsidy programs change frequently — budgets are exhausted, conditions are amended, and programs close without notice. Always verify current eligibility, amounts, and deadlines directly with the relevant authority before applying or committing capital.

ERE — Energiebesparingscertificaten (NL)

Every kilowatt-hour delivered through a public EV charger in the Netherlands generates tradeable energy savings certificates under the ERE (Energiebesparingscertificaten) scheme. This is not a subsidy you apply for — it is an ongoing revenue stream that starts the moment your charger goes live.

  • Each kWh charged on a public charger generates a certificate
  • Certificates trade at approximately €0.15/ERE (Q1 2026 market rate, manually verified)
  • A 120 kW charger delivering 200 sessions/month at 30 kWh average generates ~6,000 kWh/month = ~€900/month in certificate revenue at current rates
  • Certificates are aggregated and sold via accredited brokers — OpenCPO exports the session data required for certificate issuance
  • The rate fluctuates with energy policy — budget conservatively at €0.10/ERE for multi-year projections

THG-Quote — CO₂ Certificate Revenue (Germany)

Germany's Treibhausgasminderungsquote (THG quota) is a legal mechanism that requires fuel suppliers to offset CO₂ emissions by buying certificates. EV charging operators generate these certificates by virtue of the electricity they deliver. For 2026, the mandatory quota is set at 12.1% (up from 10.6% in 2025), driving increasing demand for certificates.

  • Applies to public chargepoints in Germany — each charging point generates certificates based on measured kWh delivered
  • Revenue varies by year and market conditions — do not assume a specific €/charger figure as it fluctuates significantly
  • Process: register your chargepoints with a THG quota service provider, they handle certification and sale, you receive payment net of their fee
  • Applies to both AC and DC public chargepoints
  • Check current rates at Umweltbundesamt (uba.de) and compare aggregator offers before committing

Country Programs

🇳🇱 Netherlands
SPRILA — Subsidieregeling Private Laadinfrastructuur bij bedrijven
Active 2026 Businesses

The main Dutch business subsidy for private on-site EV charging infrastructure. For 2026, AC and DC budgets have been merged into a single €60 million pool. The annual per-applicant cap (previously €350,000) has been removed.

AmountUp to 40% of eligible costs for SMEs; up to 20% for large enterprises. State aid rules apply.
Eligible costsCharger hardware, installation, grid connection upgrades, software integration
Minimum charger specAC charger from 2×43 kW (dual socket) or DC from 100 kW; must be publicly accessible or accessible to employees
Application window20 January 2026 – 18 December 2026
Important ruleFor applications >€25,000: apply before commissioning. For <€25,000: apply within 13 weeks after installation.
Apply atrvo.nl/sprila-aanschaf
SPULA — Subsidieregeling Publiek Toegankelijke Laadinfrastructuur
Active 2026 Public charging

Specifically for publicly accessible charging infrastructure (open to all drivers, not just employees). Minimum application is €25,000. Suitable for CPOs installing public fast chargers on semi-public sites (parking garages, retail, hospitality).

Apply atrvo.nl/spula
English infobusiness.gov.nl/subsidies-and-schemes/spula
MIA / Vamil — Milieuinvesteringsaftrek
Tax benefit Businesses

MIA and Vamil are Dutch tax schemes for environmental investments. Charging infrastructure that qualifies under the Milieulijst (environmental investment list) can be deducted at an enhanced rate from taxable profit, reducing your effective tax burden. Combined budget for 2026: €135M (MIA) + €20M (Vamil).

MIA allows deduction of a percentage of the investment above the standard depreciation. Vamil allows accelerated (arbitrary timing) depreciation — useful for managing tax liability. These stack with SPRILA.

Apply atrvo.nl/mia-vamil — application must be filed within 3 months of ordering the investment
🇩🇪 Germany
KfW — Klimaschutzoffensive für Unternehmen
Active Low-interest loans

KfW's direct grant programs for individual chargepoints have been phased out, but the Klimaschutzoffensive (Climate Protection Offensive for Businesses) offers low-interest loans for EV charging infrastructure combined with renewable energy generation. Not a grant, but favorable financing that reduces the cost of capital for larger installations.

State-level programs remain more active: Baden-Württemberg, NRW, and Bavaria have offered grants at the Bundesland level. Check with your regional Wirtschaftsförderung (economic development agency) — these programs open and close frequently.

Apply atkfw.de — via your bank (Hausbank procedure)
State programsCheck your Bundesland's energy agency for current availability
THG-Quote — Ongoing CO₂ Certificate Revenue
Ongoing revenue Public chargepoints

See the THG-Quote section above. For German-market deployments, this is a material ongoing revenue stream from day one of operation — not a one-time subsidy. Register via an aggregator service: greentrax.de, wirkaufendeinethg.de, or comparable platforms.

🇫🇷 France
ADVENIR
Changed Jan 2026

ADVENIR (funded by ADEME via EDF's CEE obligations, total budget €520M) has historically covered up to 50% of charger installation costs for businesses. As of 1 January 2026, the business charging aid component has been removed. ADVENIR remains active for residential collective installations (apartment buildings).

For commercial/CPO deployments in France in 2026, check the current ADVENIR conditions at advenir.mobi and consult ADEME directly for any replacement business programs. The French market is in transition — do not rely on ADVENIR rates from earlier years.

Workplace Charging Scheme (WCS) — OZEV
Active Businesses

OZEV's Workplace Charging Scheme provides grants toward the cost of installing EV chargepoints at workplaces. The grant has recently increased.

AmountUp to £350 per socket for installations before 1 April 2026; up to £500 per socket from 1 April 2026 onward
Max sockets40 sockets per site
EligibleUK businesses, charities, and public sector organisations at their premises
RequirementCharger must be installed by an OZEV-approved installer
Apply atgov.uk/guidance/electric-vehicle-chargepoint-grants
LEVI — Local Electric Vehicle Infrastructure Fund
Local authorities

LEVI supports local authorities in England to develop on-street residential charging. Not directly available to private CPOs, but local authority partnerships can unlock LEVI-funded infrastructure that private CPOs then operate. Check gov.uk/levi for current guidance and partner opportunities.

🇪🇸 Spain
MOVES III — Programme of Incentives for Electric Mobility
Extended — €400M Businesses + Individuals

MOVES III was extended in April 2025 with an additional €400 million (Royal Decree-law) after the previous extension was annulled. It covers both EV purchases and charging infrastructure deployment.

For businessesUp to €2.5 million per company for charging infrastructure (percentage of eligible costs — verify current rates with IDAE)
For individualsUp to 70% of home charger installation (80% in municipalities <5,000 inhabitants), max €5,000
Administered byIDAE (Instituto para la Diversificación y Ahorro de la Energía) via autonomous communities
Apply atYour autonomous community's energy agency — applications go through regional administrations

🇸🇪 Sweden
Klimatklivet — Investment Support for Climate Measures
Active Businesses + Municipalities

Klimatklivet is Sweden's primary investment support program for climate-reducing projects, including EV charging infrastructure. Administered by the Swedish Environmental Protection Agency (Naturvårdsverket). The program is competitive — projects are evaluated on cost-effectiveness (SEK per tonne CO₂ reduced).

Support levelTypically 30–65% of investment cost, maximum 70%
EligibleBusinesses, municipalities, organisations installing publicly accessible charging
Apply atnaturvardsverket.se/klimatklivet — application rounds, check current schedule
🇳🇴 Norway
Enova — Support for Fast Charging Infrastructure
Active

Enova (the Norwegian Energy Agency, state-owned) offers investment support for fast charging infrastructure. Norway is already the most EV-saturated market in the world — Enova's focus has shifted toward high-power and heavy vehicle charging. Check current call conditions at enova.no — the program structure changes with each round.

These markets have active support mechanisms, but the specific programs and percentages change frequently enough that we do not publish specific figures here to avoid outdated information. Check:

Stacking Incentives — The Full Picture

The most effective subsidy strategies combine multiple mechanisms:

  1. Capital subsidy (SPRILA, MOVES, WCS) — reduces upfront investment
  2. Tax deduction (MIA/Vamil, KfW favorable financing) — reduces effective cost of capital
  3. Ongoing certificate revenue (ERE, THG-Quote) — generates recurring income from utilization
  4. Energy revenue — the core business: margin on each kWh delivered

OpenCPO tracks session data in the format required for ERE and THG certificate issuance. Export session reports from Admin → Sessions → Export for submission to your certificate broker.
